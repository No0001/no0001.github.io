What is URL Encoding?

      
URL encoding (also known as percent-encoding) is a method of encoding special characters in a URL by replacing them with a % sign followed by two hexadecimal digits. For example, a space becomes %20 and & becomes %26.


      
Why is URL encoding needed?

      
URLs can only contain a limited set of ASCII characters. Characters like spaces, Unicode characters, and special symbols (&, =, ?, #) have special meaning in URLs. Encoding ensures they are transmitted correctly without breaking the URL structure.


      
encodeURI vs encodeURIComponent

      
    
        
  • encodeURI() ‚Äî Encodes a complete URI. It does NOT encode characters that have special meaning in a URI: : / ? # [ ] @ ! $ & ' ( ) * + , ; =

  • encodeURIComponent() ‚Äî Encodes a URI component (like a query parameter value). It encodes ALL special characters including / ? : @ & = + $ #

Use encodeURI() for full URLs and encodeURIComponent() for individual parameter values.


      
Is my data safe?

      
Yes. Everything runs locally in your browser using JavaScript's built-in encodeURI(), encodeURIComponent(), and decodeURIComponent() functions. No data is ever sent to any server.
